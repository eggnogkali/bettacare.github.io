Bettas: A Care Guide

Bettas are wonderful and easy fish to take care of. Provided that you give proper care to them, bettas should make a fantastic pet for both kids and adults alike.

Overview

The betta splendens, also known as the Siamese Fighting Fish, is a small and beautiful fish that is coveted for its vibrant colors and fancy fins. Bettas inhabit the rice paddies of tropical Southeast Asia, and have evolved a labyrinth organ to breathe air. It grows to about 2 1/4 inches, but bigger specimens have been observed. Males have much longer fins than females.

Tank Size

Contrary to popular belief, bettas will not live their best life in bowls. A 2.5 gallon filtered tank is the absolute minimun, and 5+ gallons is recommended. Males cannot be kept together, and females need at least a 10 gallon tank to cohabit.

Environment

Since bettas are tropical fish, their environment must be tropical, too. An adequate heater (5 watts per gallon) must be obtained to keep the aquarium at 78 degrees Fahrenheit. A few hides must be placed around the tank, and plants, preferably live, can be scattered throughout the aquarium. A filter must be cycled.

Conclusion

Bettas are a great introduction into the aquarium hobby, and have beautiful colors and vibrant fins. With the right equipment and mindset, they can live long, healthy, and happy lives.
